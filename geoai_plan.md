# How to identify tungsten (scheelite) in the field

## Visual identification (in the field)
- Scheelite is usually white to light gray
- Vitreous to greasy luster
- Often found in quartz veins
- Associated minerals: pyrite, arsenopyrite, tourmaline

## UV light test (most reliable for fieldwork)
- **365nm UV lamp** → scheelite fluoresces **bright blue to white**
- 254nm UV also works but weaker
- Test on a fresh surface (break the rock)

## Hardness and density
- Mohs hardness: 4.5–5 (can be scratched by a knife)
- Density: 5.9–6.1 g/cm³ (feels heavy for its size)

## Laboratory confirmation (later)
- X-ray diffraction (XRD)
- X-ray fluorescence (XRF)
- Electron microprobe

## False positives
- Some calcites and fluorites also fluoresce, but different colors (red, purple, green)
- Scheelite blue is characteristic
